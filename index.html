About ConnectWell

Our mission is to create a supportive community where healing happens through shared experiences

Our Story

Founded in 2018, ConnectWell began as a small peer support group meeting in community centers. Today, we've grown into a comprehensive behavioral health support organization serving thousands of members across the country.

Our approach is unique—we believe that those with lived experience with mental health challenges are uniquely positioned to support others on similar journeys. All our programs are designed and facilitated by individuals who have personal experience with behavioral health recovery.

Resources & Support

Tools and information to support your mental health journey

Crisis Resources

If you're in crisis, please reach out immediately:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • Emergency: 911
  • Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
Self-Care Strategies

Effective self-care practices recommended by our community:

  • Mindfulness and meditation: Practice daily meditation, even for just 5-10 minutes
  • Physical activity: Aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days
  • Sleep hygiene: Maintain a consistent sleep schedule and create a restful environment
  • Nutrition: Eat balanced meals and stay hydrated
  • Digital detox: Take regular breaks from screens and social media
  • Gratitude practice: Keep a journal of things you're thankful for
Therapy Options

Various therapeutic approaches that may help:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): Teaches skills to manage emotions and improve relationships
  • Group Therapy: Provides support from others facing similar challenges
  • Family Therapy: Involves family members in the treatment process
  • Art and Music Therapy: Creative approaches to expression and healing
Community Guidelines

Our community is built on respect, empathy, and confidentiality:

  • Respect everyone's journey and experiences
  • Maintain confidentiality - what's shared here stays here
  • Offer support without judgment or unsolicited advice
  • Practice active listening and empathy
  • Use "I" statements to share your experiences
  • Respect boundaries and personal space
  • Be mindful of triggering content and use content warnings when needed

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our programs and services

How do I join ConnectWell?

Joining ConnectWell is simple. You can sign up through our website by clicking the "Join Our Community" button. Once registered, you'll be asked to complete a brief profile to help us match you with appropriate resources and peer supporters. There's no cost to join our basic community programs.

Is there a cost for your services?

Many of our services are free, including peer support matching, community forums, and educational resources. We also offer premium programs with additional features for a monthly subscription fee. We believe in accessibility, so financial assistance is available for those who need it.

Are your peer supporters trained?

Yes, all our peer supporters complete a comprehensive training program that covers active listening, boundary setting, crisis response, and ethical guidelines. Many of our peer supporters also have professional mental health credentials in addition to their lived experience.

Is my information kept confidential?

Yes, we take privacy and confidentiality very seriously. Your personal information and what you share in support sessions is kept confidential, with exceptions only as required by law (such as risk of harm to self or others). We use secure, encrypted platforms for all our communications.

How are peer matches made?

We use a careful matching process that considers your specific experiences, needs, preferences, and goals. Our algorithm suggests potential matches, which are then reviewed by our team before being presented to you. You always have the final say in who you're matched with.

Can I become a peer supporter?

We're always looking for compassionate individuals with lived experience to join our peer support team. Requirements include at least two years in recovery/stability, completion of our training program, and a commitment to our community guidelines. Contact us to learn more about the application process.
